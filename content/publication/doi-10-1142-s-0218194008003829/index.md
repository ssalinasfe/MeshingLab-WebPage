abstract: ' Automated consistency checking of UML models becomes necessary as models
  grow in size and complexity. Since the UML metamodel does not enforce model consistency,
  there are no fixed guidelines on how to approach the consistency problem. Current
  solutions are generally partial. The translation of the metamodel and the user designed
  model into Description Logics has proved to provide a solution in detecting a large
  set of inconsistencies. In order to make this solution available to system designers,
  we have implemented MCC+, a UML model consistency checker, built as a plug-in for
  Poseidon for UML, and relying on Jena as a reasoning engine. Compared to other approaches,
  we propose a usable and scalable solution, interoperable with a known modeling tool.
  We show the application of MCC+ to a real world large example of a meshing tool. '
