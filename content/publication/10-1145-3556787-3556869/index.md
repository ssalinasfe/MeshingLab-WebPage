abstract: Current advances emphasize that young learners benefit from developing and
  applying computational thinking (CT) skills to solve problems in a variety of subjects,
  not limited to mathematics and science. Under this scenario, the inclusion of CT
  in primary and secondary education will not scale up if pre-service teachers do
  not actively acquire these skills during their formation. However, how this should
  be done is not clear. We propose an integration model to infuse CT skills at teacher
  colleges in Chile, which takes into account our previous work on the topic, as well
  as current standards, interests, and educational policies in Chile.
