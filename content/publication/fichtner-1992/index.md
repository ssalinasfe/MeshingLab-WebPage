abstract: Modern semiconductor manufacturing technology permits the integration of
  power integrated circuits with per-chip component densities reaching into the millions.
  This high-density capability, however, will only be utilized if the design costs
  can be limited to reasonable levels. Widely accepted for VLSI projects, the use
  of computer-aided design (CAD) tools has recently become a standard procedure in
  the development of power technologies and the design of new device structures and
  circuits. This paper attempts to give an overview of the status quo of CAD tools
  in power IC design, especially the use of simulation tools and computer- aided layout
  techniques. We illustrate the possibilities of these tools with examples from a
  recent MCT design effort.
