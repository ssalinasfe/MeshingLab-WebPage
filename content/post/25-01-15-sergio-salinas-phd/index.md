We are pleased to announce that **Sergio Salinas Fernández** has successfully defended his PhD thesis and graduated with his doctorate degree! 🎉

<!--more-->

## Thesis Defense

Sergio defended his doctoral thesis titled **"Polygonal/polyhedral mesh generation from Delaunay tessellations"** before a committee. His research presents approaches to mesh generation, contributing to the field of computational geometry.

## About His Research

Dr. Salinas' work focuses on developing efficient algorithms for generating polygonal and polyhedral meshes from Delaunay tessellations. This research has important applications in:

- Finite element analysis
- Computer graphics
- Scientific visualization
- Computational physics and engineering

His contributions include the development of the **Polylla algorithm**, a novel approach to mesh generation that has been implemented and made available to the research community.

## Academic Journey

Throughout his doctoral studies, Sergio demonstrated dedication and produced research outputs, including:

- Multiple peer-reviewed publications in top-tier conferences and journals
- Development of open-source software tools
- Collaboration with international researchers
- Supervision and mentorship of undergraduate students

## What's Next?

Dr. Salinas continues his work at the University of Chile as a **Professor PEX**, where he will continue advancing research in computational geometry, GPU computing, and data engineering.

## Congratulations! 🎊

The entire research group congratulates Dr. Sergio Salinas on this achievement. We look forward to his continued contributions to the field and wish him the best in his future endeavors!
